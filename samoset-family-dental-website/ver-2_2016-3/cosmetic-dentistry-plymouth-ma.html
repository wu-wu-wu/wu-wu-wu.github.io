Composite White Fillings

The days of amalgam or silver fillings are coming to an end. White fillings are placed for many reasons, whether it is to patch a broken tooth or an area of decay, we can provide you with an aesthetically-pleasing and excellently-functioning filling.

What can I expect during my appointment?

Dr. Sheng will place your new filling in one visit. She begins by preparing the affected area. Next, she cleans the prepped area and places a bonding material to adhere the white composite material to the tooth and insulate the tooth from extreme temperature changes. Then she will mix the filling composite material with coloring to match it to your true tooth color. Finally, she applies and shapes the color-matched composite material before polishing and smoothing the tooth to restore it to its original shape and function.

Once the white filling is done it will blend in with the rest of your teeth seamlessly, you won’t even notice the difference!

Lifetime Whitening

Our Lifetime Whitening program is an offer to ensure your teeth can stay whitened lifelong at a minimum cost. We make a custom tray with your teeth’s impressions and unlimited tubes of whitening material refilled every cleaning visit.

What can I expect during my appointment?

You begin with a brief appointment with our hygienist. She will take impressions of your teeth and document your current tooth shade, so that we can keep track of your progress! You then return within a week for your own custom trays tailored for your mouth and a whitening kit. We will educate you on how to effectively use the whitening materials. Then for every future cleaning visit we will address any concerns you may have and give you a refill of whitening material at no charge so you can maintain your desired smile!

Every five years you are entitled to fresh trays to ensure the maximum comfort and effectiveness at no cost!

Ceramic Crowns & Bridges

A crown is like a helmet placed over a weakened tooth providing you with the strength of a natural tooth. Best part is — we can even make it look like a natural tooth! (No one needs to know your tooth’s past and all it has been through!)

The porcelain or ceramic crowns we can provide you with are developed and prepared carefully in an excellent local lab where experienced technicians hand-craft a crown custom to your tooth’s unique shape and color. Our office goes the extra mile by being excellent in our craft in shade matching and then taking pictures to document the process of your before and after.

You’re in great hands with Dr. Sheng. She has received awards including the Dr. Remo Sinibaldi Award from the Boston University Goldman School of Dental Medicine for her outstanding achievement in fixed and removable prosthodontics, crowns, bridges and dentures.

What can I expect during my appointment?

Typically a crown will consist of two visits.

The initial visit we will prepare the tooth to fit a crown on it, take an impression, and then choose a shade that will complement the rest of your teeth. We provide you with a temporary crown to ensure the structure of your tooth remains safe and accurate. Next, we send out the impression to our specific laboratories chosen, where skilled technicians create each crown originally and to the exact specifications to your mouth.

The second and final visit is where we will place the crown, ensuring it fits to your tooth and your mouth perfectly. Once we are assured of the crowns perfections, we will cement it.

Dentures

There are three main types of dentures we are able to provide you with: complete, partial and implant supported over-dentures. The process is similar for all of them, the main difference being what condition you are in to determine what type will best suit you and your mouth.

Making dentures is something that requires detail and precision. We will take the appropriate amount of time needed in order to provide you will a comfortable and aesthetically pleasing denture.

What can I expect during my appointment?

The typical denture experience entails three — four visits.

At the first visit we will discuss your case, take impressions and determine the shade needed.

The next visit we will have try in your teeth, set in wax — essentially this is a mock-up of your future denture to ensure you are pleased with the color and overall feel.

The third visit, it is our goal to deliver you with the final denture. Of course, each case is done specifically according to the patient and unique circumstances; however we always do our best to provide patient satisfaction in a timely manner.

You will most likely return a few times in the following month in order for us to adjust the denture to your maximum comfort as your mouth adjusts to the denture.

An implant-supported denture has special attachments that snap onto attachments on the implants, making it more stable and supportive than a traditional denture that is fitted to the gums.

Root Canal Therapy

Here we have the capability and equipment to provide you with any root canal therapy treatment you may need.

We seek to ease the negative ideas associated with root canals. We assure each patient that we do everything within our power to make the procedure as pleasant as possible. Technology offers us the ability to sedate your tooth and the surrounding anatomy so that you will be able to lay back and relax during the procedure.

Our patients have even fallen asleep in the chair mid-root canal treatment!

What can I expect during my appointment?

Typically a root canal will consist of two visits. The first visit will be the actual RCT procedure where we remove the offending nerve and tissue and finish by packing the space with infection stopping material.

The next visit we will place a post and core in the tooth that will allow you to have the strength needed for the tooth to continue in its function for you.

Voilà the RCT is finished! Almost every tooth that has had root canal therapy will need a crown placed to keep the structure strong and sound. To know more about what goes into providing you with a crown click HERE.
