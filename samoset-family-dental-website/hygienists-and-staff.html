Hygienists & Staff

Sue Nogueira

Frontdesk / Assistant

Sue is the first smiling face you’ll meet in our office. She will figure out the insurance coverage for you, file the dental claims to and get the most benefit from your insurance companies. In addition, as having worked as a dental assistant for 20 years, Sue has the wealth of knowledge to explain the dental procedures you need for your dental health. In her spare time, Sue enjoys making handcraft pieces for her family and friends.

Jean Lindberg, RDH

Hygienist

Graduating from New Hampshire Technical Institute, Jean has over 20 years’ experience as a hygienist at our office. A loving wife and a mother to seven, she is a patient and caring woman. These qualities create her unique style as a hygienist. In her spare time she is busy running a household and loving her family.

Yolanda Patton, RDH

Hygienist

With nearly thirty years of experience as a hygienist, Yolanda is a valuable asset to our office. She graduated from Forsyth School of Dental Hygiene and enjoys working with her patients to maintain their teeth healthy. Yolanda is bilingual in Spanish and Portuguese. In her spare time, Yolanda likes gardening and outdoor activities, and spending time with her family.

Haley Lindberg

Assistant

Haley is a certified dental assistant who has worked in our office for two years. Being the oldest of seven children, Haley has developed a strong work ethic and an ability to communicate with all types of people. She is knowledgeable and is happy to answer your questions both inside and outside of dentistry. In her spare time, Haley volunteers at a local ranch working with horses and students. She has also worked with special needs children for over five years.

Suzanne Wu

Designer / Frontdesk

Suzanne graduated from Rhode Island School of Design with the skills of graphic and web design. Suzanne loves kids and enjoys working on computer-supported services. She joined our practice recently to strengthen our office IT services to our patients and help us at the front desk on Saturdays. Suzanne volunteers at ASPIRE, a non-profit organization to give mentorship to Asian American girls.
