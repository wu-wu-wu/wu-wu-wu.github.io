About

Suzanne is an interaction designer intent on helping people succeed.

User Experience Design | Background in Art & Furniture Design

Suzanne grew up between the idyllic countrysides of Shandong, the crowded streets of Shanghai and now the US. The scenery changed but she's remained constantly drawing. When she got accepted into art and design school at the Rhode Island School of Design she thought her ability and love for drawing meant she can only become a lone artist with crazy hair. It was at RISD she found herself in the crazy facilities at the RISD Furniture Design Department breathing sawdust and designing everyday. Within this discipline she found her passion for creating meaningful design that made real tangible difference in other people's lives, and this outcome was way better than she could have ever predicted.

Since graduating, she has worked on her craft creating marketing collaterals for various non-profits, art studios and companies like INNOVEX, Pneuhaus, and more. She also satiated her dream to work at a publishing house by developing and consuming ebooks at the MIT Press.

Suzanne recently had the privilege to work with the Fresh Tilled Soil team as a part of their User Experience Apprenticeship program. The immersion included her running her first design sprint, testing and interviewing on actual users, and strategizing business goals with user goals and presenting to clients.

Currently Suzanne is applying her design and development skills in creating humane experiences for clients in nonprofit and healthcare.
